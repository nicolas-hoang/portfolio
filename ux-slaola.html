Case Study | SLA-OLA Feature

• Deliverables: CMS Design

• Date: May, 2022

• My role: UI/UX Designer

Introduction

SLA/OLA is a feature of a B2B product called Merchant . From the business perspective, the feature can be generally understood as a method that helps the working progress more effectively, more details:

• SLA (Level Service Agreement) is an agreement between Client (Merchant) and Service Provider, which is applied to make sure Service Provider's Agents will complete their tasks on time, or they will get a penalty.

• OLA (Operational Service Agreement) is the internal agreement of a team, which is applied to make sure that team will complete the task on time.

I was the main designer working on this new feature in early Fall 2021 with a scum team of product owner, business analyst, developers, and quality controls.

(Merchant is the CRM that belongs to Client360 - the Call Center Application.)

Purpose

1. Context

Assume that one day, the Manager of KFC calls Bank of America to inquire about the current transaction because something appears to be incorrect. Because Bank of America outsourced the call center service, all calls will be received and answered by call center agents via Client360.

When the agent answers the phone, the KFC manager describes the nature of the call and requests assistance. The agent will enter the call information into the Merchant CRM and create an inquiry. When the inquiry is successfully created, it will be handled by a specific team.

2. Problem

There are probably thousands of inquiries in the CRM, and not all of them are prioritized the same way. Some are urgent and must be resolved immediately, while others can wait. However, the agent sometimes overlooks this, which has an impact on the business.

So the issue here is how to ensure that the Agent is always aware of resolving inquiries in order of priority, from most to least urgent

3. Goal

User: Complete inquiries in order of priority, from most to least urgent without missing or delay.

Business: Improve service quality.

Product: Enhance product quality by introducing a new and useful feature.

Design process

Step 1: Gathered business requirements from the Product Owner and ask questions to learn more about what the business wants, what the problem is, and so on.
Step 2: Conducted secondary research.
Step 3: Created a low-fidelity wireframe and present it to the Internal Design team, POs, and Client for feedback. This step is repeated several times until the client's requirements are met and the entire flow is approved.
Step 4: Created high-fidelity mockups and hand them off to the scrum team.
Step 5: Followed up with the scrum team and support them in case any problem happen.
Step 6: Conducted UI testing after the feature has been deployed.

Challenges

• Devoted a significant amount of time and effort to discussing and clarifying raw requirements with the PO/BA team.
• Required to understand complex business logic before and during designing phase.

Result

We received positive feedback from the business side, which mentioned that the design is clear and easy to notice when working on the task. That boosts the user's confidence because they know there is a tool that can help them with their work.
